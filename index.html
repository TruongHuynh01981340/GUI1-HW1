Home Regulations

Striped bass is a saltwater fish that many considered a good eating fish and overall, a fun fish to catch. As a result, it's a popular game fish, which means it's highly regulated in order to maintain its population. So please take a look at the regulations before learning about how to catch it.

How to catch?

To catch a striped bass is quite straight forward. Striped bass mainly eat live bait like squids, so it's recommended to use natural bait. When you cast your line, wait until you line is getting tigher. This means that the fish got your bait, so reel the line in quickly. Remember to just reel and not strike the rod as circle hook only works by reeling. When the fish is near, net the fish and measure to make sure it's a legal fish to keep.

How to measure?

Measure striped bass from the tip of its snouth if its mouth is opened, jaw if its mouth is closed to the fartest part of the tail. Once you kept a legal sized fish, you can't discard it if you caught a bigger one.
